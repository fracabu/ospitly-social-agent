## TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Font Family

**Montserrat**
- **Designer**: Julieta Ulanovsky
- **Style**: Geometric sans-serif
- **License**: Open Font License (free for commercial use)
- **Download**: Google Fonts (fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat)

**Why Montserrat**:
- Modern, clean, professional
- Excellent readability at all sizes
- Wide range of weights available
- Works well in digital environments
- Free and web-friendly
- Pairs well with supporting fonts

**Available Weights** (use these):
- **Montserrat Light (300)**: Subtle headers, elegant large text
- **Montserrat Regular (400)**: Body text, captions
- **Montserrat Medium (500)**: Subheadings, emphasized text
- **Montserrat SemiBold (600)**: Headings, important text
- **Montserrat Bold (700)**: Headlines, CTAs, strong emphasis
- **Montserrat ExtraBold (800)**: Large impact text (use sparingly)

### Secondary Font Family (Optional Pairing)

**Open Sans**
- **Style**: Humanist sans-serif
- **Usage**: Alternative body text for long-form content
- **Weights**: Light (300), Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)

**When to Use**:
- Blog posts (better for long reading)
- Email newsletters
- Long-form captions on Facebook
- Body text in PDFs/guides

### Accent Font (For Elegance)

**Playfair Display**
- **Style**: Serif, elegant, classic
- **Usage**: Rare accent for luxury/elegance
- **Weights**: Regular (400), Bold (700)

**When to Use**:
- Quotes from customers
- Elegant headers for special content
- Italian hospitality heritage posts
- Use sparingly (accent only, not body text)

### Typography Scale

**Social Media Text Hierarchy**:

**Hero/Display Text** (Instagram/Facebook featured graphics):
- **Font**: Montserrat Bold or ExtraBold
- **Size**: 48-72pt (scale for canvas size)
- **Line Height**: 1.1-1.2
- **Use**: Main headline, attention-grabbing statement
- **Color**: Teal, White (on dark), or Charcoal

**Headline** (Primary heading):
- **Font**: Montserrat Bold
- **Size**: 36-48pt
- **Line Height**: 1.2
- **Use**: Post titles, section headers
- **Color**: Teal or Charcoal

**Subheadline**:
- **Font**: Montserrat SemiBold or Medium
- **Size**: 24-32pt
- **Line Height**: 1.3
- **Use**: Supporting headlines, subtitles
- **Color**: Charcoal or Teal (lighter weight)

**Body Text**:
- **Font**: Montserrat Regular (or Open Sans Regular)
- **Size**: 18-24pt (must be readable on mobile)
- **Line Height**: 1.5-1.6
- **Use**: Paragraphs, descriptions, captions
- **Color**: Charcoal (#2D3748)

**Caption/Small Text**:
- **Font**: Montserrat Regular or Light
- **Size**: 14-16pt
- **Line Height**: 1.4
- **Use**: Image captions, credits, fine print
- **Color**: Charcoal (slightly reduced opacity 70-80%)

**CTA/Button Text**:
- **Font**: Montserrat Bold or SemiBold
- **Size**: 18-24pt
- **Letter Spacing**: +1-2% (slightly increased for impact)
- **Use**: Call-to-action buttons, important links
- **Color**: White (on colored button) or Teal

### Text Styling Best Practices

**Readability**:
- Minimum font size: 18pt for body text on graphics (mobile readability)
- Maximum line length: 50-75 characters for body text
- Adequate line spacing: 1.5x font size minimum
- Avoid all-caps for long text (hard to read)
- Use sentence case for most text (not Title Case)

**Emphasis**:
- **Bold**: Primary emphasis (Montserrat SemiBold or Bold)
- **Italic**: Subtle emphasis (use sparingly in social media)
- **Color**: Emphasis through brand colors (teal, terracotta)
- **Size**: Hierarchy through size differences

**Alignment**:
- **Left-aligned**: Default for paragraphs, natural reading
- **Center-aligned**: Headlines, quotes, symmetric designs
- **Right-aligned**: Rarely (only for specific design balance)
- **Justified**: Avoid in social media (creates uneven spacing)

**Special Characters**:
- Em dash: â€” (not two hyphens)
- Quotes: "curly quotes" (not "straight")
- Ellipsis: â€¦ (single character, not three periods)
- Apostrophes: ' (curly, not straight)

**Italian Language**:
- Ensure font supports Italian characters: Ã , Ã¨, Ã©, Ã¬, Ã², Ã¹
- Montserrat supports full Latin character set
- Test Italian text before finalizing

## LOGO USAGE

### Primary Logo

**Logo Variations**:

**Full Logo - Horizontal**:
- Ospitly icon + "Ospitly.it" wordmark side-by-side
- **Use**: Headers, Facebook cover, website, print
- **Minimum width**: 120px (digital), 0.75 inches (print)
- **Orientation**: Horizontal lockup

**Logo Mark (Icon Only)**:
- Ospitly icon without text
- **Use**: Profile pictures, app icons, social media avatars, small spaces
- **Minimum size**: 40x40px (digital), 0.5 inches (print)
- **Works**: At very small sizes where text would be illegible

**Stacked Logo**:
- Icon above wordmark (centered)
- **Use**: Vertical layouts, narrow spaces, square formats
- **Minimum height**: 80px (digital), 1 inch (print)

### Logo Clear Space

**Clear Space Rule**:
- Maintain minimum clear space around logo equal to the height of the "O" in Ospitly
- No text, graphics, or other elements within this space
- Ensures logo visibility and impact
- Applies to all sides (top, bottom, left, right)

**Visual Guide**:
```

    |
    v
[----O----]
    OSPITLY.IT
[----O----]
    ^
    |
[Clear Space = "O" height]
```

### Logo Color Variations

**Primary - Full Color**:
- Icon: Teal (#0F766E)
- Wordmark: Charcoal (#2D3748)
- **Use**: Default on white/cream backgrounds
- **Best for**: Most applications

**Reversed - White**:
- Icon: White (#FFFFFF)
- Wordmark: White (#FFFFFF)
- **Use**: On dark backgrounds (photos, colored backgrounds)
- **Minimum background darkness**: 60% for visibility

**Monochrome - Black**:
- Entire logo: Black (#000000) or Charcoal (#2D3748)
- **Use**: Black and white printing, faxes (rare), formal documents

**Monochrome - White**:
- Entire logo: White (#FFFFFF)
- **Use**: Dark backgrounds, high contrast scenarios

### Logo Placement Guidelines

**Social Media Profile Pictures**:
- Use icon-only logo mark
- Ensure icon is centered in circular crop
- Test at smallest size (40x40px)
- High contrast for visibility in feeds

**Social Media Covers/Banners**:
- Use full horizontal logo
- Place in safe zone (not covered by profile picture)
- Size appropriately (not too large, not too small)
- Typically top-left, top-right, or bottom-right

**Content Graphics**:
- Small logo watermark in corner (bottom-right typically)
- 20-30% opacity for subtlety
- Size: Small but visible (50-80px width)
- Protects content from unauthorized use

**Video Content**:
- Logo watermark bottom-right corner (persistent)
- Full logo on opening/closing frames (2-3 seconds)
- Size: 10-15% of frame width

### Logo Don'ts

**NEVER**:
- âŒ Stretch or distort logo (maintain aspect ratio)
- âŒ Rotate logo (keep horizontal/stacked orientation)
- âŒ Outline logo (no stroke/border)
- âŒ Apply drop shadows or effects (keep clean)
- âŒ Use on busy backgrounds (reduce background visibility or add background shape)
- âŒ Change logo colors (use approved variations only)
- âŒ Rearrange logo elements (icon and text positioning is fixed)
- âŒ Animate logo excessively (simple fade or scale in only)
- âŒ Place logo on low-contrast backgrounds (ensure visibility)
- âŒ Use outdated or unofficial logo versions

### Logo Files Organization

**File Naming Convention**:
- `ospitly-logo-horizontal-color.png` (full color, horizontal)
- `ospitly-logo-horizontal-white.png` (white, horizontal)
- `ospitly-logo-icon-color.png` (icon only, color)
- `ospitly-logo-icon-white.png` (icon only, white)
- `ospitly-logo-stacked-color.png` (stacked, color)

**File Formats Needed**:

- **JPG**: Solid background, photos, email signatures
- **EPS/AI**: Vector, print use, designers

**Resolution**:

- **Print**: 300 DPI minimum
- Provide multiple sizes: Small (500px), Medium (1000px), Large (2000px)

## PHOTOGRAPHY & IMAGERY

### Photography Style

**Aesthetic Direction**: Warm, Authentic Italian Hospitality

**Characteristics**:
- **Lighting**: Natural light preferred, warm golden-hour tones
- **Color Temperature**: Warm (2800-4500K), Mediterranean feel
- **Composition**: Balanced, not overly staged, authentic moments
- **Focus**: Slightly shallow depth of field for professional look
- **Editing**: Warm tones enhanced, slight saturation boost, increased warmth

**Subject Matter**:

**Properties & Spaces**:
- Beautiful Italian accommodations (hotels, B&Bs, vacation rentals)
- Architectural details highlighting Italian character
- Interior spaces: Warm, inviting, well-lit
- Exterior shots: Italian landscapes, cityscapes, countryside
- Avoid: Generic stock hotel images, international chain aesthetic

**People & Hospitality**:
- Property owners and managers (authentic, not models)
- Genuine host-guest interactions
- Team members working (natural, candid)
- Italian hospitality professionals
- Diverse age ranges (representing all personas)
- Avoid: Overly posed, fake smiles, stock photo feel

**Technology & Product**:
- Ospitly platform interface (clean screenshots)
- People using platform (laptop, tablet, mobile)
- Work environments (offices, hotel front desks, on-the-go)
- Modern technology in hospitality context
- Avoid: Generic tech stock photos, non-Italian settings

**Italian Context**:
- Italian locations and landmarks (not clichÃ©)
- Local food and wine (hospitality culture)
- Italian lifestyle and culture
- Regional diversity (North to South Italy)
- Seasons and occasions (tourism cycles)
- Avoid: Tourist clichÃ©s (leaning tower, gondolas in every post)

### Photography Guidelines

**Resolution & Quality**:
- Minimum: 1920x1080px (Full HD)
- Preferred: 4K (3840x2160px) for future-proofing
- Format: JPG (high quality, 90%+) or PNG (if transparency needed)
- File size: Optimized for web (<500KB per image ideally)

**Composition Rules**:
- **Rule of Thirds**: Place key elements on intersecting lines
- **Leading Lines**: Use architectural or natural lines to guide eye
- **Negative Space**: Don't clutter - leave breathing room
- **Symmetry**: Use for impact and professionalism
- **Depth**: Foreground, middle, background for dimension

**What to Capture**:
- Wide shots: Establish location and context
- Medium shots: Show interaction and activity
- Close-ups: Details that tell story, emotional connection
- Candid moments: Authentic over posed
- Variety: Horizontal, vertical, square for different platforms

### Image Editing Guidelines

**Color Grading** (Lightroom/Photoshop):
- Increase warmth (+5 to +15 temperature)
- Slight saturation boost (+5 to +10)
- Increase vibrance (+10 to +20) for natural color enhancement
- Lift shadows slightly for softer look
- Subtle vignette for focus
- Clarity: +5 to +10 for crispness

**Preset Style** (create custom preset):
- Name: "Ospitly Italian Warmth"
- Temperature: +10
- Tint: 0 to +5 (slightly magenta for warmth)
- Exposure: Adjust per image
- Contrast: +5 to +10
- Highlights: -5 to -10 (recover blown areas)
- Shadows: +10 to +15 (lift for softer look)
- Whites/Blacks: Adjust per image for proper exposure
- Vibrance: +15
- Saturation: +5
- Tone Curve: Slight S-curve for contrast
- HSL: Boost oranges/reds for warmth, desaturate blues slightly
- Split Toning: Warm highlights (orange), cool shadows (blue) for dimension
- Sharpening: +40
- Noise Reduction: As needed

**Consistency**:
- Use same preset for all brand photos
- Batch editing for content series
- Save presets for quick application
- Before/after comparison to ensure style consistency

**Photo Overlays & Text**:
- When adding text to photos, ensure readability
- Use overlays: Semi-transparent color (teal, black) at 40-60% opacity
- Gradient overlays: Dark at bottom for text placement
- Avoid: Text directly on busy areas without background

## GRAPHIC DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Layout & Composition

**Grid System**:
- Use grid for alignment and consistency
- 12-column grid for flexibility
- Margins: Minimum 5-10% of canvas width
- Gutter space: Consistent throughout design
- Align all elements to grid for cohesion

**Visual Hierarchy**:
1. **Primary**: Most important element (headline, CTA) - largest, boldest, high contrast
2. **Secondary**: Supporting information (subheadline, key points)
3. **Tertiary**: Additional details (captions, fine print)
- Size, weight, color, and position establish hierarchy

**White Space (Negative Space)**:
- Essential for clean, modern designs
- Don't fill every pixel - let content breathe
- Margin minimums: 10% of canvas on all sides
- Space between elements: At least 20-30px
- White space = sophistication and readability

**Balance**:
- **Symmetrical**: Formal, stable, professional (use for corporate content)
- **Asymmetrical**: Dynamic, interesting, modern (use for engaging content)
- **Radial**: Elements arranged around center point (use for focus)
- Choose based on content and message

**Alignment**:
- Align elements along common axis
- Avoid random placement (creates chaos)
- Left-aligned text: Most readable for paragraphs
- Center-aligned: Headlines, quotes, symmetrical designs
- Right-aligned: Rarely, for visual balance
- Grid helps maintain alignment

### Graphic Elements

**Icons**:
- **Style**: Outlined or flat (consistent throughout brand)
- **Weight**: 2-3px stroke for outlined icons
- **Size**: Consistent sizing for same context
- **Color**: Brand colors (teal, terracotta, charcoal)
- **Sources**: Font Awesome, Feather Icons, Heroicons, custom
- **Usage**: Illustrate concepts, guide eye, add visual interest

**Shapes & Patterns**:
- Simple geometric shapes (circles, rectangles, triangles)
- Rounded corners: 8-16px border-radius (modern, friendly)
- Avoid harsh right angles unless intentional
- Patterns: Subtle, not overwhelming (10-20% opacity)
- Italian-inspired patterns: Terrazzo, Mediterranean tiles (subtle)

**Lines & Dividers**:
- Thickness: 1-3px for dividers, 4-8px for decorative
- Color: Brand colors or 20% opacity gray
- Style: Solid, dashed, dotted (choose one per design for consistency)
- Usage: Separate sections, guide eye, add structure

**Backgrounds**:
- **Solid**: Clean, professional (cream, white, brand colors)
- **Gradient**: Modern, depth (teal to light teal, cream to white)
- **Image**: Ensure text readability (overlay, blur, darken)
- **Texture**: Subtle paper or canvas texture (10-20% opacity) for warmth
- **Pattern**: Very subtle (5-10% opacity) for interest without distraction

### Data Visualization

**Charts & Graphs** (for statistics and results):
- **Colors**: Use brand palette consistently
  * Positive data: Success Green (#059669)
  * Negative/problem data: Alert Red (#DC2626)
  * Neutral data: Teal (#0F766E) or Charcoal (#2D3748)
- **Style**: Clean, modern, minimal
- **Labels**: Clear, readable (Montserrat)
- **Simplicity**: Remove unnecessary elements (gridlines, borders)
- **Accessibility**: Patterns or textures in addition to color (for colorblind)

**Common Chart Types**:
- **Bar Charts**: Comparisons (before/after, Ospitly vs competitors)
- **Line Graphs**: Trends over time (growth, improvement)
- **Pie Charts**: Proportions (booking sources, time savings)
- **Icons + Numbers**: Simplified statistics for social media
- **Progress Bars**: Goal achievement, percentage improvements

**Infographic Style**:
- Vertical flow for storytelling (top to bottom)
- Icons + short text for scannability
- Brand colors with white space
- Numbered steps or logical progression
- Mobile-friendly (readable on phone)

## VIDEO GUIDELINES

### Video Style & Aesthetic

**Overall Direction**: Professional yet approachable, with Italian warmth

**Characteristics**:
- **Quality**: HD minimum (1080p), 4K when possible
- **Framing**: Stable, well-composed (tripod or gimbal)
- **Lighting**: Natural or three-point lighting (flattering, clear)
- **Audio**: Clean, clear (external microphone essential)
- **Pacing**: Engaging (not too slow, not frenetic)
- **Length**:
  * Social media: 15-60 seconds (Reels), 3-10 minutes (IGTV/Facebook Video)
  * Platform demos: 10-15 minutes
  * Customer testimonials: 60-90 seconds

### Video Types & Specifications

**Instagram Reel**:
- **Resolution**: 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical)
- **Length**: 15-90 seconds (optimal: 30-45 seconds)
- **Format**: MP4
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps minimum, 60fps for smooth motion
- **Orientation**: Vertical (fills screen)
- **Audio**: Trending sounds OR original with voiceover
- **Subtitles**: Essential (80% watch without sound)

**Facebook Feed Video**:
- **Resolution**: 1280x720 (720p) minimum, 1920x1080 (1080p) preferred
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 (landscape), 1:1 (square), 4:5 (vertical)
- **Length**: 1-10 minutes (optimal: 1-3 minutes)
- **Format**: MP4 or MOV
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps
- **Subtitles**: Uploaded as SRT file or burned-in

**Instagram Story Video**:
- **Resolution**: 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical)
- **Length**: Maximum 15 seconds per clip
- **Format**: MP4
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps
- **Orientation**: Vertical (full-screen)
- **Subtitles**: Burned-in (brief, large text)

**YouTube (For longer content)**:
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 (1080p) or 3840x2160 (4K)
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 (landscape)
- **Length**: Varies (tutorials 10-20 min, demos 15-30 min)
- **Format**: MP4
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps or 60fps
- **Subtitles**: Upload separate SRT file

### Video Production Guidelines

**Pre-Production**:
- Script or outline (key points, CTA)
- Storyboard or shot list
- Location scouting (quiet, good lighting, on-brand)
- Equipment check (camera, microphone, lighting, tripod)
- Talent preparation (interview questions, talking points)

**Production (Shooting)**:
- **Camera Settings**:
  * Manual focus (prevents hunting)
  * Manual white balance (consistent color)
  * Highest quality codec
  * Log format for color grading (if capable)
- **Audio**:
  * External microphone (lapel or shotgun)
  * Backup audio recorder (phone)
  * Monitor audio levels (-12dB to -6dB)
  * Minimize background noise
- **Framing**:
  * Rule of thirds
  * Headroom: One eye-height above head
  * Looking space: Space in direction of gaze/movement
  * Multiple angles (establish, medium, close-up)
- **Lighting**:
  * Three-point lighting (key, fill, back)
  * Natural lighting supplemented
  * Soft, diffused light (flattering for faces)
  * Consistent temperature (daylight 5600K or tungsten 3200K)

**Post-Production (Editing)**:
- **Software**: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve, iMovie
- **Editing Style**:
  * Fast pacing (cuts every 3-5 seconds)
  * Jump cuts acceptable (remove filler words)
  * B-roll cutaways (visuals supporting narration)
  * Text overlays for key points
  * Brand intro/outro (2-3 seconds each)
- **Color Grading**:
  * Apply Ospitly color LUT (warm, vibrant)
  * Consistent color across clips
  * Slight warmth boost (+5 temperature)
  * Vibrance +10-15
- **Audio**:
  * Normalize audio levels
  * Remove background noise (noise reduction)
  * Add background music (low volume, 10-20% of dialogue)
  * Sound effects (subtle transitions, emphasis)
- **Graphics**:
  * Animated logo intro (2-3 seconds)
  * Lower thirds (name, title) - Montserrat font, brand colors
  * Text overlays (key points, statistics) - large, readable
  * CTA overlay (last 5 seconds) - clear, actionable
- **Subtitles**:
  * Hardcode subtitles (burned into video) OR upload SRT
  * Font: Montserrat Bold, white text, black background bar
  * Placement: Bottom third, centered
  * Timing: Appear with speech, readable duration
  * Accuracy: Proofread carefully

   - Gather team feedback

   - Share best practices and learnings

### When to Update Brand Identity

**Minor Updates** (Allowed):
- Seasonal color variations (subtle)
- New template types (maintaining core style)
- Optimization based on performance data
- Platform-specific adaptations

**Major Updates** (Rare, Requires Approval):
- Logo redesign (avoid unless necessary)
- Primary color palette change (brand recognition impact)
- Complete typography overhaul
- Fundamental style shift

**Change Process**:
1. Document reason for change
2. Get stakeholder buy-in
3. Create transition plan
4. Update all materials simultaneously
5. Announce changes to community
6. Archive old brand assets (for reference)
